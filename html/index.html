Celebrating SP70th Anniversary


Singapore Polytechnic (SP), known for being the first polytechnic in Singapore, was first established in 1954 on Prince Edward Road. By 1974,the construction of the campus on Dover Road began and was completed in 1978. On 7th July 1979, the campus on Dover Road was officially opened by Prime Minister Lee Kuan Yew.

SP70 Music Festival

We celebrated our 70th anniversary with a music festival at SP. Despite the rain, thousands of attendees danced, especially during performances by local artists Marian Carmel, Rangga Jones, and Evan Low. The festival highlighted the importance of youth mental health and showed that mental health and music can go hand in hand.

Being life-ready, work-ready, and world-ready is our mission, which embodies the essence of SP's DNA of becoming "future-ready." This passage from our commemorative book highlights three areas in which SP is setting the standard for progress:



Personal/Work Life

Personal/Work Life

Both personal and professional spheres are changing dramatically. Global connectivity is a fundamental component of SP's "future-ready" DNA. This involves encouraging an interest in the rest of the world and all of its diversity. Together with increasing one's ability to comprehend the needs of users, consumers, markets, and audiences, an international perspective also fosters empathy for individuals from different cultural backgrounds, which is essential in most professional domains.


Industry Disruptions

Industry Disruptions

With revolutionary advancements in fields like artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning, the Internet of Things (IoT), and robotics, the entire industrial sector has grown considerably more disruptive. In order to remain competitive in the future, SP must welcome and adjust to changes in the industry, whether they come from advancements in technology or other changes in the workplace. The only way to protect yourself from the worst kind of disruption and displacement is to prepare for the future.


Global Shifts

Global Shifts

For the majority of people, climate change is the most important global issue currently facing the world. At Singapore Polytechnic, this subject is very important because efforts to make students more "future-ready" center on addressing sustainability, always from a comprehensive, big-picture perspective. One of the ten Common Core Curriculum modules, the Polytechnic's Sustainable Innovation Project (SIP), is where this commitment is most evident. Students work on this learning experience in their second year of study, culminating in a capstone project.
